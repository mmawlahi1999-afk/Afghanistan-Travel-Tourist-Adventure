Guardians of History

A journey through 5,000 years of civilization, art, and poetry.

The Crossroads of Asia

Afghanistan has been a focal point of the Silk Road for centuries. From the Greek influence in Balkh to the Islamic architecture of Herat, every stone tells a story.

Minaret of Jam

UNESCO World Heritage

Standing at 65 meters tall in a remote valley of Ghor province, this 12th-century masterpiece is renowned for its intricate brickwork and blue glazed tile decoration.

Citadel of Herat

The Heart of Khurasan

Also known as Qala Ikhtyaruddin, this imposing fortress dates back to Alexander the Great. It has been restored to showcase the grandeur of the Timurid empire.

Bamyan Cultural Landscape

Art of Gandhara

The Bamyan Valley represents the artistic and religious developments which from the 1st to the 13th centuries characterized ancient Bakhtria, integrating various cultural influences.
